Website Evaluations

CRAP stands for Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, and Proximity. These principles help judge whether a design is clear, organized, and easy to use.

Optical Toys

Well designed

  • Contrast: This site uses strong color and visual effects that make it stand out. The moving optical designs grab attention quickly.
  • Repetition: The site repeats a similar visual style throughout, so the page feels consistent and intentional.
  • Alignment: The layout is simple and centered, which makes the page feel organized even though the visuals are busy.
  • Proximity: Related items are grouped close together, so it is easy to tell what belongs together.

Badger Badger Badger

Poorly designed

  • Contrast: This site has a black background color and white text. This is an example of bad contrast. When the background is too dark or too light, it can be difficult to read the text.
  • Repetition: The site does not repeat a similar visual style throughout, so the page feels inconsistent and unintentional.
  • Alignment: The page contains multiple sections that do not feel visually connected. The large animation dominates the page and makes it difficult for users to focus on the navigation and other content.
  • Proximity: The animation occupies most of the page while supporting links and information are separated above and below it, making the page feel disconnected.
